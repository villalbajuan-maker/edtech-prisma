# Problem Hypothesis

## Mother Hypothesis

Higher education institutions lose students, resources, and intervention capacity because they do not have an early, integrated, and actionable diagnostic reading of incoming students.

Although institutions often have administrative, academic, and financial data, many relevant risk signals are only detected once they appear as low grades, absence, financial arrears, withdrawal, or disengagement.

This late and fragmented detection makes it difficult to segment students properly, prioritize institutional support, and direct permanence resources toward those who need them most.

## Refined Problem Statement

The problem is not simply student dropout.

Dropout is the visible outcome.

The deeper operational problem is that many institutions do not convert student entry into an early permanence strategy.

They admit, enroll, and administratively classify students, but they do not always diagnostically understand their preparation, expectations, motivation, constraints, support networks, and early fragility conditions.

## Strategic Wound

The institution knows the student administratively before it knows the student diagnostically.

PRISMA Permanencia exists to close that gap.
