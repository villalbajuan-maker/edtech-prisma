# Microproblem Forensics

This file focuses on the three most powerful initial microproblems for PRISMA Permanencia.

## 1. Late Detection

### Hypothesis

The institution detects risk when the student has already started failing, missing classes, accumulating financial pressure, isolating, or emotionally disengaging.

### Institutional Failure

Many early warning systems are based on the first visible negative data point:

- low grades;
- absence;
- financial arrears;
- course cancellation;
- low platform use;
- teacher reports;
- withdrawal requests.

These are early administrative signals, but often late human signals.

### Forensic Chain

```text
Administrative entry
-> undiagnosed expectations
-> university shock
-> silent early difficulty
-> low performance or absence
-> institutional alert
-> late contact
-> student already disengaged
-> withdrawal, pause, or fragile permanence
```

### What The Instrument Must Do

The instrument must surface entry conditions before formal negative data exists.

It must identify:

- initial fragility;
- mismatch between expectations and reality;
- doubts about program choice;
- weak study habits;
- financial pressure;
- low support network;
- limited time availability;
- low belonging;
- low help-seeking confidence.

## 2. Lack Of Actionable Segmentation

### Hypothesis

Institutions may detect that a student is at risk, but they cannot always differentiate what type of risk the student has or what action corresponds to that risk.

### Institutional Failure

"At-risk student" is too broad to be operational.

Different students need different interventions:

- academic leveling;
- vocational orientation;
- financial support;
- time-load planning;
- mentoring;
- institutional integration;
- individual follow-up.

### Forensic Chain

```text
General risk detected
-> dominant cause not identified
-> generic support offered
-> student does not receive the needed support
-> low adherence
-> risk continues
-> institution concludes the student did not use support
```

### What The Instrument Must Do

The instrument must classify:

- priority level;
- dominant fragility;
- secondary fragility;
- profile;
- recommended intervention route;
- urgency;
- institutional owner.

## 3. Poor Allocation Of Institutional Support

### Hypothesis

Institutions often have support programs, but they lack a precise mechanism to connect each student to the right support, at the right moment, with the right level of priority.

### Institutional Failure

Support is often distributed through general campaigns, voluntary attendance, teacher referrals, or late alerts.

Students with the greatest need may not be the ones who ask for help.

### Forensic Chain

```text
Institution has support services
-> no fine-grained diagnostic need
-> generic support offer
-> right students do not arrive
-> teams serve demand, not priority
-> high-risk students remain invisible
-> intervention arrives late or never arrives
```

### What The Instrument Must Do

The instrument must operate as an initial routing engine:

```text
Student response
-> vulnerable dimension
-> profile
-> priority
-> recommendation
-> institutional area
-> suggested action
-> follow-up
```

## Product Implication

PRISMA Permanencia must not stop at diagnosis.

It must convert diagnosis into:

- segmentation;
- prioritization;
- institutional routing;
- recommended action;
- responsible use language;
- follow-up logic.
