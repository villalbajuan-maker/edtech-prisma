# Higher Education Colombia

## Initial Market Focus

Colombian universities and higher education institutions.

## Target Institutions

Initial attractive profiles:

- private universities;
- mid-sized IES;
- institutions with technical, technological, and professional programs;
- institutions with visible pressure around permanence;
- institutions with welfare or permanence teams but limited diagnostic segmentation;
- institutions with virtual or hybrid programs;
- institutions with scholarship, financing, or vulnerable student populations.

## Buyer Map

Potential economic buyers:

- rector;
- academic vice-rector;
- student affairs vice-rector;
- permanence director;
- welfare director;
- planning director;
- quality/accreditation director;
- dean;
- program director.

Operational users:

- permanence team;
- welfare team;
- academic coordinators;
- faculty leadership;
- financial support office;
- tutoring and leveling teams.

## Economic Pain

The cost of poor early diagnosis appears through:

- lost tuition revenue;
- wasted acquisition cost;
- inefficient use of permanence resources;
- late interventions;
- weaker retention and graduation indicators;
- reputational damage;
- low confidence in permanence strategy;
- poor allocation of welfare, academic, and financial support.
