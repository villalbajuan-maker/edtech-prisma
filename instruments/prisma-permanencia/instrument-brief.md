# PRISMA Permanencia Instrument Brief

## Name

PRISMA Permanencia

## Working Definition

PRISMA Permanencia is a digital diagnostic instrument for higher education institutions that identifies preparation, early fragility, permanence risk, and accompaniment needs in incoming students.

## Purpose

Help institutions move from late reaction to early, segmented, and actionable permanence management.

## Target Population

Incoming higher education students:

- admitted students;
- enrolled first-semester students;
- students in critical programs;
- students in virtual or hybrid programs;
- students with scholarships or financing;
- first-year cohorts.

## Core Institutional Question

Which students need support, what type of support do they need, and how should the institution prioritize accompaniment before late risk signals appear?

## Diagnostic Logic

The instrument identifies:

- preparation conditions;
- expectation alignment;
- academic habits;
- time and external load;
- financial fragility;
- support networks;
- belonging;
- non-clinical emotional adaptation;
- permanence intention;
- dominant risk profile.

## Outputs

Student-level outputs:

- dimension levels;
- priority of accompaniment;
- dominant profile;
- key fragility signals;
- recommended route.

Institution-level outputs:

- cohort map;
- program/sede/modality comparisons;
- profile distribution;
- priority groups;
- recommended institutional actions;
- export for support teams.

## Ethical Boundary

PRISMA Permanencia does not predict dropout as a deterministic label.

It identifies conditions of fragility and priority of accompaniment.
